# Two-Layer Boundary Condition

## The Photon Field (CMB)

- Decoupling epoch: z ≈ 1090, t ≈ 380,000 years
- Temperature today: T_γ ≈ 2.725 K
- Fluctuation amplitude: ΔT/T ≈ 10⁻⁵
- Fully mapped: Planck satellite, angular resolution to ℓ ≈ 2500
- Angular power spectrum peaks: ℓ ≈ 220, 540, 810 (acoustic oscillations)

## The Neutrino Field (CνB)

- Decoupling epoch: z ≈ 6×10⁹, t ≈ 1 second
- Temperature today: T_ν = (4/11)^(1/3) × T_γ ≈ 1.945 K
- Never directly detected (interaction cross-section vanishingly small at these energies)
- Imprint on CMB: phase shift of ~0.5° in acoustic peaks, measurable in Planck data
- Planck constraint: N_eff ≈ 3.046 (consistent with 3 neutrino flavors)
- Detection attempt: PTOLEMY experiment (Princeton), targeting tritium beta decay endpoint

## Orthogonality

The two fields are orthogonal because:
- Different force carriers: electromagnetic (photon) vs weak force (neutrino)
- Different Hilbert subspaces at decoupling
- Cannot be continuously deformed into each other (topologically inequivalent)
- Both are horocycles sharing the same ideal boundary point (the singularity)

This is not metaphorical orthogonality. It is the orthogonality of distinct quantum field sectors.

## The Seed

The Big Bang singularity is the shared ideal boundary point of both horocyclic fields.

- No observer has causal access to it
- Both fields point toward it but neither reaches it
- It is the null centroid: present in every trajectory as a limit, absent from every observation
- The seed signature (tan window) is the divergence between the two fields — where they differ most is where the singularity's initial asymmetry is most legible

## The Antiverse

The antiverse is the mathematical inversion of the combined boundary condition. It is:
- Physically impossible (no causal mechanism)
- Metaphorically precise (the universe the seed did not choose)
- Ontologically empty but geometrically well-defined

It is not the neutrino field. The neutrino field is real.
