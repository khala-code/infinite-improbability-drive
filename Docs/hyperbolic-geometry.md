# Hyperbolic Geometry of the Boundary

## Wavefronts as Horocycles

In hyperbolic space (the natural geometry of an expanding universe with Λ > 0), geodesics diverge exponentially. Wavefronts propagating from the boundary are horocycles — curves orthogonal to all geodesics in a given direction, parallel to the absolute boundary.

In the Poincaré disk model, horocycles are circles internally tangent to the boundary disk. They share an ideal boundary point — in this engine, that point is the Big Bang singularity.

The photon and neutrino wavefronts are both horocyclic families centered on the same ideal point, at different hyperbolic depths.

## The Zeta Line

The zeta geodesic is the axis of maximum informational propagation — the radial geodesic from the observer to the singularity in hyperbolic space. All wavefront horocycles are parallel to the zeta line (they share its ideal boundary point).

Wavefront surfaces are orthogonal to the observer worldline and parallel to the zeta geodesic. The Za coordinate measures hyperbolic depth along this geodesic.

## Ω as Conformal Scale

The Penrose diagram compactifies infinite spacetime into a finite causal diamond. The observer bubble selects a conformal patch of this diamond scaled by the Ω parameter.

In AdS (Anti-de Sitter) space with metric:

    ds² = (L²/z²)(-dt² + dx² + dz²)

the AdS radius L corresponds to Ω, and z corresponds to Za. The bulk-to-boundary propagator:

    K(z, x; x') = z^Δ / (z² + (x-x')²)^Δ

describes how a boundary wavefront at x' reaches bulk point (z, x). Its level surfaces are horocycles.

Different Ω values are different conformal gauge choices — all physically equivalent up to scale. Larger Ω means more wavefronts intersect the observer bubble, richer structure visible.

## Two Orthogonal Horocyclic Families

Photon and neutrino fields are two horocyclic families sharing the same ideal point but at different hyperbolic distances from the boundary. They cannot be continuously deformed into each other — this is the non-orientable surface property analogous to a Klein bottle: the two sides cannot be consistently assigned inside/outside.
