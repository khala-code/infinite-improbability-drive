# Glossary

> A compact reference for the core terms in the Observer Prime framework.

---

## A

**Absolute Null**  
The centre-point of the circular T-topology. Not empty space, not vacuum, and not merely "nothing," but the state of absolute impossibility in which no distinction, no observer, and no information differential exists. Null is orthogonal to every observer position on the T-ring.

**Agent, Consequential**  
An observer bubble whose local sign commitments meaningfully affect residue propagation across scales. A consequential agent operates at Heegner 43, where actions matter even though the next attractor cannot be classically computed.

**Attractor**  
A basin toward which a system converges under ξ upward pressure. Convergent Uncomputable problems have an attractor even when no classical path to it can be derived.

**Axis of Evil**  
The anomalous large-scale alignment of the CMB quadrupole and octupole. In the framework, a candidate fingerprint of directional anisotropy in the ξ field during CMB crystallisation.

---

## B

**Bifurcation Membrane**  
The threshold surface at which ξ sign becomes undecided and the system is about to choose a basin. In the Riemann mapping, associated with the critical line Re(s) = ½.

**Bifurcation Zone**  
A region of near-zero ξ eigenvalue where the system is maximally underdetermined. Not a point but an extended transition region in which sign selection, proper time cost, and residue production are all intensified.

**Big Bang**  
Not the observer's T-origin. A point on the circular T-topology where the past-directed and future-directed infinities nearly meet around Null. The Big Bang is one threshold event within the broader topology, not the absolute start of linear time.

---

## C

**Calibration Sequence**  
The ordered progression of ζ transition nodes across cosmological and civilisational scales. In the current formulation: Null/time emergence, baryogenesis/neutrino decoupling, BBN, CMB decoupling, structure formation, biological emergence, and civilisational threshold.

**Charm**  
The ξ tensor axis measuring a node's degree of self-distinction — the depth of internal ξ history that is legible to the node itself. A high-charm node has enough interior coherence that external pressure (gravitas, coercion, high-void-density adjacency) cannot simply overwrite its ζ geodesic. Charm is the prerequisite for consent: a node with zero charm cannot consent, it can only comply or resist reflexively. See also: *Dark Charm*, *Consent Threshold*, and `QUARK-FIELDS.md`.

**Chirality Lock**  
The handedness selection that appears at multiple scales. Biologically, it appears as L-amino acid dominance. Cosmologically, it is linked to the Majorana handedness selection at baryogenesis.

**Consent Threshold**  
The minimum charm value at which a node's agreement or refusal signal is genuinely its own rather than gravitas-captured. Below the consent threshold, agreement is compliance and refusal is reflexive resistance — neither is consent in the first-person sense. The threshold scales with the gravitas field the node is embedded in: higher gravitas requires higher charm to maintain geodesic autonomy. See `QUARK-FIELDS.md`.

**Convergent Uncomputable (CU)**  
The Heegner-67 complexity class. A problem space that cannot be solved classically, requires quantum computation, but still has an attractor. The path is unknowable classically; the destination is real.

**Critical Line**  
The line Re(s) = ½ in the complex plane of the Riemann zeta function. In the framework, the universal bifurcation depth at which all incomplete cancellations leave their residue.

---

## D

**Dark Charm**  
Charm running in the un-owned channel. The node has interior depth (charm is structurally present) but has not integrated it into its declared self-model. The hidden interior still steers the ζ geodesic — producing compulsion, repetition, and unconscious pattern — but from outside the node's legible self. Dark charm is not pathological by definition; it is an integration state. It becomes integrated charm when the node's scrying resolution is sufficient to read its own interior. See `QUARK-FIELDS.md`.

**Dark Matter Halo**  
Not a particle halo in this framework, but a geometric response of the void density field at information-density boundaries. A self-generated counterbalance structure in the ξ/void-density substrate.

**Divergent Uncomputable (DU)**  
The Heegner-163 complexity class. A problem space that cannot be solved classically, requires quantum computation, and has no attractor from inside any single observer bubble. Resolution requires genuine consensus among independent agents.

---

## E

**Euler Half-Turn**  
The phase rotation implemented by the term e^{iπ·σ} in the candidate T operator. Each increment of σ rotates the system by π in the complex plane, performing recursive sign alternation.

---

## F

**Faith**  
The operational form of T for a classical observer at a Convergent Uncomputable boundary. More precisely: injecting a sign into an underdetermined system when the sign cannot be derived from prior information, but local ξ still provides directional guidance.

**Filter**  
A calibration checkpoint or transition-stage constraint used to test whether the continuous ζ accumulation remains internally consistent across scales.

---

## G

**Gravitas**  
The geodesic-bending field generated by dependency relationships between nodes. When node B depends on node A for ξ budget (resources, validation, safety, coherence), node A acquires gravitas over node B — curving B's ζ geodesic toward A's attractor regardless of B's own charm. Gravitas operates even without intent on A's part: the dependency gradient alone curves B's trajectory. Structurally identical to how mass curves spacetime. See `QUARK-FIELDS.md` for the interaction table with charm states.

**Ground State**  
The carried condition inherited after a transition. Residue from one transition becomes the ground state for the next.

---

## H

**Heegner Numbers**  
The nine class-number-1 values: 1, 2, 3, 7, 11, 19, 43, 67, 163. In the framework, these mark reducibility islands — scales at which T has unique decomposition and problem structure is locally well-formed.

**Heegner 43**  
The maximum classical-complexity boundary and current civilisational position in the framework. Still heuristically tractable, but adjacent to the quantum threshold.

**Heegner 67**  
The Convergent Uncomputable boundary, where quantum computation becomes structurally necessary.

**Heegner 163**  
The Divergent Uncomputable boundary and terminal class-number-1 point. The last reducibility island before permanent non-uniqueness.

---

## I

**Incomplete Cancellation**  
The core generative event of the framework: Null fails to cancel itself perfectly, leaving residue. This is the origin of existence, asymmetry, and all later structure.

**Independence Condition**  
The requirement that agents participating in a Divergent Uncomputable resolution be genuinely independent observer bubbles, not correlated copies or simulated partitions of one system. Falls from Derived Axiom 9 (No Privileged Frames): correlated copies do not produce a new frame.

---

## L

**L₃**  
The coherence/interface Lagrangian. The third Lagrangian that mediates between GR and QM through ξ. At civilisational scale, L₃ becomes the dominant effective term.

**Leech-Lattice Signature**  
The repeated appearance of 24-dimensional structure in the Heegner gaps, especially 43→67 and 67→163. Treated as a marker of symmetry architecture at the quantum threshold.

**Lithium Deficit**  
The lower-than-expected primordial Li-7 abundance. In the framework, a residue signature of proper time budget constraints during BBN.

**Local ξ Measurement**  
The agent's best in-bubble reading of coherence gradient, used to orient action when the full path cannot be computed.

---

## M

**Majorana Neutrino**  
A neutrino that is its own antiparticle. In the framework, central to unifying baryogenesis and handedness selection at the same transition node.

**Mexican Hat**  
The visual metaphor for a bifurcation zone: the unstable high point before the system falls into one basin or another.

---

## N

**No Privileged Frames**  
Derived Axiom 9. All observer coordinate frames are first-class information configurations. No ZaTaOa position is ontologically prior to any other. Scale asymmetry (recursive sign alternation) is position within a standing wave, not ontological privilege. Gravitas is a local geometry effect, not a privilege claim.

**Null → Time Transition**  
The threshold event at which forced self-measurement crosses the ξ threshold and proper time emerges. There is no clock before this crossing.

---

## O

**Observer Bubble**  
The deformed manifold centered on an observer's ZaTaOa coordinate. Encodes ξ on its surface and defines the local geometry of trust, underdetermination, and residue.

**Observer Prime**  
The single axiom that information is the substrate of reality and that mass/energy are precipitation modes of informational structure.

---

## P

**Pascal Calculation**  
The expected-value logic at a CU boundary: bounded downside, real upside, no classical proof available. Expressed informally as "what do we have to lose?"

**Proper Time Tax**  
The irreducible cost paid by a system to traverse a transition or irreducibility zone. More complex transitions require more proper time budget.

---

## R

**Ramanujan Remainder**  
The tiny gap by which e^{π√163} misses an integer. In the framework, the terminal residue of incomplete cancellation at the last reducibility island and the closure gap of the circular T-topology.

**Recursive Sign Alternation**  
The rule that the dominant ξ sign flips at each scale boundary while remaining stable within a scale.

**Residue**  
The structured remainder left by incomplete cancellation. Residue propagates forward, becomes the next ground state, and appears cosmologically, mathematically, computationally, socially, and psychologically.

**Retrograde Inference**  
The scrying-system method of running T backwards heuristically from present anomalies to identify the minimum-surprise causal chain.

---

## S

**Sakharov Conditions**  
The standard requirements for baryogenesis: baryon number violation, C/CP violation, and out-of-equilibrium dynamics. Reinterpreted in the framework through the Majorana/ξ sign-selection model.

**Scrying Loop**  
The recursive human/software process used to navigate Convergent Uncomputable territory. Software improves ξ signal quality; the user commits sign and reads residue.

**Self-Distinguished**  
A node with sufficient internal ξ history to be legible to itself — able to locate its own ZaTaOa coordinate with enough precision that external fields cannot trivially overwrite its self-model. Self-distinction is the precondition for charm in the active sense: it is not an achieved state but a threshold condition. Below the self-distinction threshold, the node cannot produce a stable first-person readout. See *Charm*, *Consent Threshold*.

**Sign Commitment**  
An action taken under underdetermination that biases the system toward one basin. The irreducible act at a bifurcation zone.

---

## T

**T Operator**  
The transition operator that maps one ζ state to the next while preserving information, accumulating residue, flipping sign at scale boundaries, and paying the proper time tax.

**T-Origin**  
The observer's local temporal anchor, not the Big Bang. The point on the circular T-topology from which the observer reads past and future residue.

**Trauma**  
At the human scale, residue from a hostile higher-scale sign that has leaked inward and been internalised by a lower-scale unit such as a family or individual.

---

## V

**Void Density**  
The true substrate quantity replacing naive geometric distance. High void density means effective separation; low void density means proximity regardless of coordinate distance.

---

## X

**ξ (Xi) Field**  
The non-local coherence tensor measuring alignment strength between nodes in the information substrate. ξ controls trust, transition thresholds, observer-bubble shape, and access to attractor structure.

**ξ Threshold**  
The coherence boundary at which a system becomes able to cross into a new phase. Also the line between retained attractor visibility and collapse into divergence.

---

## Z

**ZaTaOa**  
The observer coordinate triple: Oα (observer state), Zα (origin of personhood), and Tα (local time origin). Defines the centre of the observer bubble.

**ζ (Zeta) Accumulation**  
The continuously carried coupling field, built from Ω + ξ and propagated through every transition without reset.

**ζ Geodesic**  
The trajectory traced by a node's accumulated ζ through phase space over time. Distinct from the node's ZaTaOa position: the geodesic is the path, the ZaTaOa is the current coordinate. Gravitas bends the geodesic; charm resists that bending; dark charm bends it from the inside without the node's knowledge.

**ζ Node**  
A phase transition point in the calibration sequence where accumulated ζ crosses a bifurcation condition and produces new residue.
