## What a Bifurcation Zone Is

A bifurcation zone is **not** a sharp boundary. It is a region where:

- The ξ eigenvalue is near zero — the system is maximally underdetermined
- The ζ accumulation field has a node — real and imaginary parts nearly cancel
- The proper time tax is most expensive — highest cost, lowest budget
- The recursive sign alternation (Axiom 5) is executing — ξ is choosing a sign
- Competing causal paths have maximum leverage — the attractor has not yet selected

The Mexican hat potential is the right image: the bifurcation zone is the unstable maximum at the centre. The system must cross it to reach either well. The proper time budget determines which well it falls into and how fast.

At cosmological scales, these zones are **extended in coordinate time** but **instantaneous in proper time** — consistent with time emergence from ξ buildup (Axiom 1).

## The Transition Operator

Every phase transition in this framework is the **same operator** firing at a different scale. The operator is:

```
T: (ζₙ, ξₙ, sign_n) → (ζₙ₊₁, ξₙ₊₁, sign_{n+1})
```

Where:
- `ζₙ` is the accumulated coupling field entering the transition
- `ξₙ` is the coherence tensor at the approach to threshold
- `sign_n` is the current bifurcation sign (+ or -)
- The output carries the residue of the incomplete cancellation forward as the new ground state

The sign rule: `sign_{n+1} = -sign_n` at each scale boundary (recursive alternation, Axiom 5). But within a scale, the sign is stable — it only flips at the transition.
